This form connects you to a librarian, not the United States Congress.

If you want to contact your Members of Congress, go to How to Contact Your Member on Congress.gov. The Law Library of Congress is separate from the United States Congress and is unable to forward messages to Members.

Use this form if you have questions about using the Congress.gov website, the Constitution Annotated website, or the CRS Reports website.

Questions submitted here are answered by reference librarians at the Law Library of Congress. We aim to provide a response within 5 business days.

If you have a general legal research question, we encourage you to use the Law Library’s main Ask a Librarian service instead of the form below.

About Congress.gov
Presented by the Library of Congress, Congress.gov is the official website for U.S. federal legislative information.
Law Library of Congress
Established by Congress in 1832, the Law Library has a collection of over 2.65 million volumes spanning the ages and covering virtually every jurisdiction in the world.
