2025 Gold Medal Summit

June 11 - June 13

  • « 2025 ‘Chips and Chiefs’ Charity Poker Tournament
  • 2025 Golf Classic »

June 11 – 13, 2025 | Washington, D.C.

Congratulations, Class of 2025 Gold Medalists! We’re thrilled to invite you to this year’s summit, where we’ll celebrate your remarkable accomplishments and inspire you to keep reaching new heights. If you plan to attend, please submit your RSVP by April 20, 2025.

Register for the 2025 Gold Medal Summit

If you won’t be joining us in person, there’s no need to RSVP. Your medal will be sent to you or your local Congressional office closer to the ceremony, based on the preference you selected in your online Record Book submission. In the meantime, if you’d like to share your success with your school or local press, check out our School/Press notification form.

Wednesday, June 11th – Friday, June 13th, 2025
Washington, D.C.
Add to your calendar:

Registration Deadline

Please note that all RSVPs must be submitted by April 20, 2025, 11:59 p.m. local time. Unfortunately, we cannot accept late submissions.

Summit Refund & Cancellation Policy

  • Cancellations received before May 1, 2025 are refundable, minus a $50 fee for processing and administrative costs.
  • Cancellations on or after May 1, 2025 are non-refundable.
    We appreciate your understanding; this policy ensures we can finalize event logistics in a timely manner.

Summit Agenda

While we’re still finalizing details, the 2025 Summit will feature a wide range of activities:

  • Welcome Reception where you can meet other Gold Medalists and alumni.
  • Congressional Meetings to recognize your achievements on Capitol Hill.
  • Engaging Workshops & Panels featuring influential leaders from government, business, and civic sectors.
  • Washington Nationals Baseball Game & On-Field Access for a unique D.C. experience.
  • Local Attractions & Tours highlighting the city’s iconic sites and cultural events.

You can find a snapshot of the tentative three-day itinerary below. Please remember that the schedule is subject to change. We’re working hard to confirm additional speakers, venues, and activities to make sure you get the most out of your time in D.C.!

Day One - Wednesday, June 11, 2025
Welcome Day will have activities taking place in the afternoon and evening, giving medalists and their guests time to arrive and get settled in before a full day of programming tomorrow.
 
  • Welcome reception & check-in event to meet your fellow medalists and get your Medalist Welcome Bag
  • Group activities & ice breakers
  • Sightseeing and local attractions
Day Two - Thursday, June 12, 2025

Hill Day will include a full day of programming in the U.S. Capitol from the morning to the evening.

  • Kick-off breakfast
  • Recognition meetings and special access with Members of Congress in and around the U.S. Capitol
  • Speaking panels in the Capitol Visitor Center’s Congressional Auditorium
  • Lunch in the U.S. Capitol Cafe
  • Access to Congressional Award Lounge area in the Capitol Visitor Center with refreshments, photo opportunities, merchandise, and staff available to assist medalists
Day Three - Friday, June 13, 2025
Nats Day will kick off at the ballpark with special on-field access and provide freedom for medalists to “choose their own adventure” with exclusive discount codes and tailored recommendations for a variety of activities around the city, including shows, museums, and tours.
 
  • Washington Nationals on-field experience
  • Sightseeing and local attractions
  • Washington Nationals baseball game

Hotel Accommodations

If you’d like to stay with fellow Medalists, we’re currently securing a hotel block in Washington, D.C. There are plenty of other nearby accommodations as well, so feel free to shop around. We’ll share all hotel block details as soon as they’re finalized.

Event App – Coming Soon!

You’ll be able to access the full agenda, receive real-time updates, and connect with other attendees in one convenient spot.

Event FAQs

We’ve compiled answers to the most frequently asked questions about the Summit—from logistical details and dress code to meeting schedules and more.

FAQs

If I am only available for certain days or have conflicts with some of the agenda items, can my schedule be personalized?
We understand that everyone has unique schedules and commitments. Unfortunately, due to the nature of the event and the number of attendees, as well as the schedules of the Members of Congress who will be participating, it is not possible to personalize schedules for individual participants based on their own availability. We are working to coordinate with the Members of Congress to maximize their availability during the Summit and to create a comprehensive and engaging itinerary for the event.
 
We recommend blocking off three full days (or a half-day for the first day of the Summit) to fully participate in all the Summit has to offer. Your meeting with your Member of Congress will be scheduled with you closer to June.
When do I need to submit my record book by to be included in this event?

The deadline to submit your online record book to be included in the 2025 Gold Medal Class was February 1, 2025. If revisions are needed, the deadline to submit additional information or corrections to your Record Book is March 15, 2025 at 11:59 pm local time. Submit your record book here and find helpful tips, guides, and resources for submitting your record book here.

If I don't attend the Summit, how will I get my medal?

Medals will be mailed to medalists who are unable to attend the Summit after the event takes place in June. We will attempt to set up local presentations for any medalists who who have indicated they would like one in their Record Book submission.

Will travel costs or accommodations be covered?

Travel costs and accommodations will not be covered by The Congressional Award. 

Are we allowed to bring guests?

Registration for the Gold Medal Summit is priced at $600 per medalist + processing fees. Each medalist has one (1) guest included in their registration. Medalists can add a second guest (Gold Medalist + 2 guests total) for a $200 additional guest fee. This fee covers certain meals, tickets, venue costs, and access to all Summit events.

Please note that due to our large group and venue capacity, we are NOT able to make exceptions to the guest allowance. Again, unfortunately we do not have the capacity for additional guests, including young children.

Will meals be covered?

Light refreshments will be provided throughout each day as well as a breakfast buffet, lunch vouchers, and meal voucher at the Nationals game for the Gold Medalist and their registered guests.

Will scholarships/financial aid be available?

We are excited to offer a registration fee waiver for the 2025 Gold Medal Summit to those who may need financial assistance. In the event registration form, you will find an application form for a waived registration fee. Please note that this waiver will only cover the $600 registration fee for the medalist and 1 guest, and does not include flights, hotels, or supplementary meals. A committee will review all requests for a waiver on a need-basis, however, please be aware that the number of slots available is limited. If your application is not approved, you will be required to provide payment later on in order to attend the Summit.

Am I guaranteed a meeting with my Member of Congress?

Congressional meetings will take place throughout day 2 of the Summit (June 12, 2025) in various locations within the Capitol complex. While we are unable to guarantee that your Representative and/or Senator(s) will be available due to the ongoing legislative and voting schedule, we will begin the scheduling process for you ASAP and attempt to get your meeting(s) on the schedule.

Please note that Congressional office staff will be scheduling meeting(s) directly with you and your fellow medalists, so please keep an eye on your email and respond promptly when they reach out in a few weeks to coordinate a time and place.

If you do not request a meeting on the event registration form, we will not reach out to schedule a meeting on your behalf. In lieu of a Congressional meeting, you will have the opportunity to attend all of the expert speaking panels throughout the day. Regardless, there will be many opportunities to meet with legislative, business and civic leaders throughout the Summit. We will work with you to arrange meeting opportunities in recognition of earning your Gold Medal.

I have special needs/need handicap accessibility. Will this be taken into account at the event?

Please be sure to indicate if you and/or your guest(s) need assistance coordinating accessibility accommodations when filling out the event registration form so we can provide the necessary information to our venues. We are more than happy to assist with your needs in order to ensure a seamless and enjoyable experience.

Most of the events of the 2025 Gold Medal Summit will take place in The U.S. Capitol Visitor Center (CVC) as well as the Senate and House of Representative Office Buildings, which all offer a variety of services for individuals with disabilities. Read more about the CVC accessibility services here, and the House/Senate Office Buildings here. Supplementary events will take place at local hotels, sporting event venues, and local museums or event spaces. As these venues are secured, we will be sure to prioritize sending out information on ADA compliance and accessibility services. Our team will be ready to assist any individuals with special needs.

Will there be a formal Gold Medal Ceremony like in years past?

Due to the increasing number of medalists, in addition to restrictions in the Capitol, the 2025 Gold Medal Summit will not include a formal ceremony. We have shifted to an in-person summit model for medalists to meet with their Members of Congress in an environment that encourages conversation and interaction.

Will previous Gold Medal classes be able to attend?

Unfortunately, we will not be able to extend an invitation to medalists beyond the 2025 class at this time.

However, we would still like to offer you a few opportunities to stay involved as an alumni member of the program:

  1. We invite you to consider applying to become a Diversity Ambassador, a role that provides opportunities to promote diversity, equity, and inclusion within the Congressional Award community.
  2. We encourage you to join our alumni network on LinkedIn, where you can connect with fellow medalists and stay informed about upcoming events and career opportunities.
  3. We also encourage you to check out the resources in our Career Center, where you can find valuable tools and guidance to help you pursue your career goals and find job opportunities from our corporate partners.
What is the agenda?

Please refer to the brief snapshot of the itinerary included at the beginning of this webpage. Attendees will be notified once the agenda is finalized in the coming weeks.

We look forward to celebrating your achievements as you continue Reaching New Heights at the 2025 Gold Medal Summit. Stay tuned for more updates, and don’t forget to RSVP by April 20—we can’t wait to see you in D.C.!
