UPC ROOTS

UPC ROOTS

Share on Facebook Share on X Print this Page Share by Email
, 2025

Uganda Congress Peoples (UPC) has always indicated in a number of its documents that it is a social democratic party. The roots of Uganda Peoples Congress (UPC) can be traced to the Uganda National Congress (UNC), which was formed in 1952 under the leadership of Ignatius Musazi the UNC emerged out of Uganda African farmers Union (UAFU) which was a movement of small holder farmers agitated for better prices of coffee and cotton.

At the time, the main economic and political activities were concentrated in Buganda and naturally the leadership of the day. With time, the Baganda lost out on the political leadership because they lacked the local base and support of Mengo established. This, therefore, changed the ethnic and character of the leadership of the Party.

In terms of ideological outlook, UNC was generally socialist in orientation. There was reason why UNC adopted a socialistic stance. In order to be able to negotiate for better prices the UNC leadership sought the assistance of Fener Brokway sent John Stone house, another socialist to help in organizing both farmers' cooperative movement association and UNC itself. Stonehouse was also able to arrange for Ignatius Musazi to take time off, both Soviet Union for assistance basically because they were fighting colonialism which was perpetrated by the western world..
